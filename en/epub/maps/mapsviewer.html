Move the map

This feature is enabled by default. In case you used any other navigation tool, click on the corresponding icon in the toolbar to return to move mode, then move the map by holding your cursor support.

Zoom by rectangle selection

To zoom by rectangular selection, click on the corresponding icon in the tool bar and then click on the map and draw a rectangle by dragging your cursor. This causes the zoom of the map on the geographical scope defined by the rectangle.

Note

You can also zoom by rectangular selection by pressing the Shift key on your keyboard and then drawing a rectangle on the map

Zoom to the maximum extent

To zoom to the maximum extent of the map, click on the corresponding icon in the toolbar. This causes the zoom of the map on the global scope (ie -180, _90, 180, 90).

Zoom to a predefined scale

The dropdown list at the top right of the viewer allows you to zoom to predefined scales. Clicking on a scale drives the zoom of the map corresponding to the level of zoom.

Zoom Slider

The zoom slider at the top left of the viewer allows you to zoom and move the map. Click on one of the directional arrows in the top of the map moves in the corresponding direction.

Note

You can also move the map by holding your cursor pressing on the “joystick” in the center of the top of the slider and moving the

Click on the toolbar zoom levels set in the bottom of the slider causes the zoom of the map to the corresponding level.

Note

You can also click on a predefined zoom level (continent, country, city, house) to the right of the slider bar

OpenStreetMap importer

The OpenStreetMap data importer to import items ( “nodes”) and lines ( “ways”) from the OpenStreetMap database.

Icon

 Action

Opens the Import window OpenStreetMap data

To open it, click on the corresponding icon in the Viewer toolbar.

Then follow the steps to start the import:

  • Draw a rectangular area on the map

  • Select then the online mode or bitmap for import

  • Select one of the: doc: ../ data / datastores. available from the dropdown list in which the imported data will be stored

  • Specify a name for the new layer

  • Click the “Import” button

After a few seconds, the line item or type of data is displayed on the map and the new layer is added to the tree layers. You can use this layer like any other.

Warning

The more you select a large geographical area, the longer the waiting time is long when importing. It is advisable to use the importer to an adequate zoom level manner as not to cause too long queries.
